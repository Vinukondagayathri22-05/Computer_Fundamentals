# Introduction to Computers

## 1. What is a Computer?

A computer is an electronic device that accepts data as input, processes it according to a set of instructions, stores it, and produces meaningful information as output.

### Definition

A computer is a programmable machine that performs arithmetic and logical operations automatically and efficiently.

### Basic Working of a Computer

```text
Input → Processing → Output
         ↓
       Storage
```

### Example

* Input: Marks entered by a teacher
* Processing: Calculation of total and average
* Output: Student result
* Storage: Saved in a database or file

---

## 2. Characteristics of Computers

### 1. Speed

Computers can perform millions or billions of operations per second.

Example:

* Calculating complex equations within seconds.

### 2. Accuracy

Computers produce accurate results if the instructions and input data are correct.

Example:

* Banking transactions.

### 3. Diligence

Computers do not get tired or bored.

Example:

* Running continuously in data centers.

### 4. Storage Capacity

Computers can store large amounts of data.

Example:

* Hard disks storing terabytes of information.

### 5. Automation

Computers can perform tasks automatically once programmed.

Example:

* Automatic backup systems.

### 6. Versatility

Computers can perform multiple types of tasks.

Example:

* Gaming, programming, video editing, and scientific research.

### 7. Reliability

Computers can consistently perform operations without failure.

Example:

* Air traffic control systems.

## 5. Limitations of Computers

### No Intelligence

Computers cannot think independently.

### No Decision-Making Ability

They only follow programmed instructions.

### Dependence on Humans

Require human input and programming.

### No Emotions

Computers cannot understand feelings.

### Garbage In, Garbage Out (GIGO)

Incorrect input leads to incorrect output.

Example:
If wrong marks are entered, wrong results will be generated.

## 6. Generations of Computers

Computers have evolved through five generations.

### First Generation (1940–1956)

#### Technology

Vacuum Tubes

#### Features

* Very large size
* High power consumption
* Generated much heat
* Slow compared to modern computers

#### Examples

* ENIAC
* UNIVAC I

---

### Second Generation (1956–1963)

#### Technology

Transistors

#### Features

* Smaller size
* Faster processing
* Lower power consumption
* More reliable

#### Examples

* IBM 1401
* IBM 7094

---

### Third Generation (1964–1971)

#### Technology

Integrated Circuits (ICs)

#### Features

* Smaller and cheaper
* Increased speed
* Improved reliability

#### Examples

* IBM System/360

---

### Fourth Generation (1971–Present)

#### Technology

Microprocessors

#### Features

* Personal computers emerged
* High processing speed
* Compact size
* Affordable

#### Examples

* Desktop PCs
* Laptops

---

### Fifth Generation (Present and Future)

#### Technology

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

#### Features

* Machine learning
* Natural language processing
* Robotics
* Intelligent systems

#### Examples

* AI Assistants
* Self-driving vehicles
* Expert systems

---

## Summary

* A computer is an electronic device that processes data into information.
* Main operations: Input, Processing, Storage, and Output.
* Characteristics include speed, accuracy, diligence, storage, and automation.
* Computers are used in education, healthcare, banking, business, and research.
* Advantages include speed, accuracy, and large storage.
* Limitations include lack of intelligence and dependence on human instructions.
* Computers have evolved through five generations from vacuum tubes to AI-powered systems.
