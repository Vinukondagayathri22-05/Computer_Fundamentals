# Computer Networks Basics

## Introduction

Modern computers are often connected to each other to share information and resources. This connection of computers is called a network.

Computer networks enable communication, data sharing, internet access, and resource sharing among multiple devices.

---

# What is a Network?

## Definition

A Computer Network is a collection of two or more computers and devices connected together to share data, resources, and services.

## Examples

* Internet
* College Computer Lab
* Office Network
* Home Wi-Fi Network

---

# Advantages of Computer Networks

* Resource Sharing
* File Sharing
* Internet Sharing
* Fast Communication
* Data Backup
* Cost Reduction

# Types of Networks

Networks are classified based on geographical area covered.

```text id="st8jz6"
Computer Networks
│
├── LAN
├── MAN
└── WAN
```

---

# 1. LAN (Local Area Network)

## Definition

A LAN is a network that connects computers within a small geographical area.

## Coverage

* Home
* School
* College
* Office Building

## Features

* High speed
* Low cost
* Easy maintenance

## Example

A computer lab with 30 connected computers.

## Advantages

* Fast communication
* Easy file sharing

## Disadvantages

* Limited coverage area

---

# 2. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)

## Definition

A MAN connects multiple LANs across a city or metropolitan area.

## Coverage

* City
* Large campus
* Multiple office branches

## Features

* Larger than LAN
* Moderate speed
* Covers several kilometers

## Example

A university connecting multiple campuses in a city.

## Advantages

* Connects large organizations

## Disadvantages

* More expensive than LAN

---

# 3. WAN (Wide Area Network)

## Definition

A WAN connects computers and networks across large geographical areas such as countries or continents.

## Coverage

* Country
* Continent
* Worldwide

## Features

* Very large coverage
* Connects multiple LANs and MANs

## Example

The Internet

## Advantages

* Global communication

## Disadvantages

* Higher setup cost
* Lower speed compared to LAN

---

# Comparison of LAN, MAN, and WAN

| Feature   | LAN                | MAN                       | WAN               |
| --------- | ------------------ | ------------------------- | ----------------- |
| Full Form | Local Area Network | Metropolitan Area Network | Wide Area Network |
| Coverage  | Small Area         | City                      | Country/World     |
| Speed     | High               | Medium                    | Lower             |
| Cost      | Low                | Medium                    | High              |
| Example   | School Lab         | City Network              | Internet          |

---

# Internet

## Definition

The Internet is the largest computer network in the world that connects millions of computers globally.

## Features

* Global connectivity
* Information sharing
* Online communication
* Access to websites and services

## Services

* Email
* Web Browsing
* Video Calls
* Online Learning
* Cloud Storage

---

# Intranet

## Definition

An Intranet is a private network used within an organization.

## Features

* Restricted access
* Internal communication
* Secure environment

## Examples

* College portal
* Company employee portal

## Advantages

* Secure communication
* Easy information sharing

---

# Extranet

## Definition

An Extranet is an extension of an intranet that allows authorized external users to access certain resources.

## Features

* Controlled access
* Secure sharing
* Collaboration with external users

## Examples

* Supplier access systems
* Partner portals

---

# Internet vs Intranet vs Extranet

| Internet         | Intranet                  | Extranet                   |
| ---------------- | ------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Public Network   | Private Network           | Semi-Private Network       |
| Open to Everyone | Organization Members Only | Authorized External Users  |
| Global Access    | Internal Access           | Controlled External Access |

# Common Networking Devices

## Router

Connects networks and provides internet access.

### Example

Home Wi-Fi Router.

---

## Switch

Connects multiple devices within a LAN.

### Example

Office network switch.

---

## Hub

Connects devices but broadcasts data to all devices.

### Example

Older network setups.

---

## Modem

Converts digital signals for internet communication.

### Example

Broadband internet connection.

# Summary

* A network connects computers and devices.
* LAN covers a small area.
* MAN covers a city.

* Extranet allows limited external access.

# Interview Questions

### Q1. What is a Computer Network?

A collection of interconnected computers and devices that share resources and information.

### Q2. What are the advantages of computer networks?

* Resource sharing
* File sharing
* Communication
* Internet access

### Q3. What is LAN?

A network covering a small geographical area such as a school or office.

### Q4. What is MAN?

A network covering a city or metropolitan area.

A network covering large geographical areas such as countries or the world.

WAN.

A private network within an organization.

A network that allows authorized external access to an organization's resources.

Internet is public, while Intranet is private.
